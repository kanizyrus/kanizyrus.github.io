What I think about

AI & Software Development

I'm not interested in AI hype. I'm interested in what happens when you hand real engineers real AI tools and watch what they actually do with them. The gap between the demo and the daily workflow is where the interesting work lives.

Engineering Leadership

I spent years being the person who wrote the code. Now I'm the person who builds the team that writes the code. The transition was harder than any technical problem I've faced, and I'm still learning.

Game Development

I've loved games since I was a kid. Not just playing them — understanding how systems interact, how incentives shape behavior, and why the most interesting bugs in games are usually design problems in disguise.

Fintech

I founded Pymlo and learned firsthand what it means to handle other people's money. Regulated technology demands a discipline that move-fast culture doesn't prepare you for. I still think about these problems.
