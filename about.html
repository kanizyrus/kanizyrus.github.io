About

Ping-Hsien Lin (Kaniz)

I'm an engineering leader who has spent the better part of a decade building software systems and the teams around them. I've led cross-functional engineering organizations, founded a fintech company, and shipped products across domains ranging from financial infrastructure to developer tooling to AI-assisted workflows.

My work sits at the intersection of several things: building distributed systems that hold up under real-world pressure, leading engineering teams through ambiguity, and writing about what I notice when things start to go sideways — in code, in organizations, in thinking.

I'm particularly interested in applied AI — not the hype cycle, but the unglamorous work of embedding intelligence into existing systems and workflows in ways that actually help people work better. I serve as an AI ambassador because I believe the most important AI work is happening in integration, not invention.

Before my current work, I founded a fintech company where I learned that building regulated technology demands a very different kind of discipline than Silicon Valley's move-fast ethos prepares you for. That experience shaped how I think about trust, systems design, and the weight of handling other people's money.

I also think a lot about game systems — not just as entertainment, but as a lens for understanding incentive structures, emergent behavior, and the design problems that only surface when complex agents interact with your carefully laid plans.

This site is where I think out loud. The essays are attempts to make sense of what I observe; the projects are experiments in building things that reflect that thinking.
