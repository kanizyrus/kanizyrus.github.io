About me

My name is Ping-Hsien Lin, and most people call me Kaniz. I'm a Taiwanese software engineer who has been living in Bangkok, Thailand since 2012.

I studied Computer Science at National Taiwan University. My first jobs were in embedded systems and ERP consulting — about as far from the startup world as you can get. Those early years taught me that most software problems aren't really about software. They're about people, incentives, and communication.

In 2014, I co-founded Pymlo, a fintech startup that built lending infrastructure. We built the platform from scratch, navigated Thai financial regulations, and learned more about trust, risk, and the weight of handling other people's money than I ever expected to. The company didn't become a unicorn, but it taught me things that no amount of reading could.

Today, I lead a chapter of full-stack developers in a global enterprise. I also serve as an AI Ambassador, which means I spend a lot of my time helping engineering teams figure out where AI tools actually help and where they just add noise. I try to stay close to the code even as my role becomes more about people and strategy.

Outside of work, I'm interested in game development (as a systems design lens, not just entertainment), photography, bass guitar, Japanese language and culture, and the general project of trying to get a little better at things every day.

This site is where I write. Not to build an audience or establish thought leadership — mostly to think. Writing forces me to figure out what I actually believe versus what I've just been repeating. If something here is useful to you, that's a bonus.
